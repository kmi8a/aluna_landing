The Origin of ALUNA

Where intention
becomes form.

In Tayrona thought, Aluna is the invisible dimension where intention precedes form — where thought, memory, and order arise before anything takes shape in the world.

ALUNA The Frequency of Being translates that understanding through water, sound, light, and human interaction.

What the Tayrona knew as cosmological principle, cymatics makes physically visible: vibration organizes matter. Sound gives form to what was formless. The name and the mechanism describe the same truth.

Through interaction, that truth becomes tangible. What you choose to emit — the frequency you set, the intensity you bring — directly shapes what appears. Vibration affects matter. Presence shapes perception. The invisible organizes experience.

That is not only what the sculpture demonstrates. It is what it asks you to consider about yourself.

Not only a sculpture — an encounter with the unseen forces that move through us and around us.

Why It Matters

The invisible
organizes experience.

ALUNA is built on a principle that extends far beyond the sculpture itself.

Cymatics demonstrates something verifiable and profound: vibration organizes matter. What cannot be seen still shapes what can. Sound, frequency, and wave — forces we normally experience only as sensation — leave a physical imprint on the world.

The piece extends that principle outward. If vibration shapes water, it may also shape what we carry and what we emit — the quality of our presence, the energy of our words, the resonance of our attention.

The Artwork

Material
memory.

Standing 12 feet tall on the playa, ALUNA draws its visual language from Tayrona goldwork and the myth of El Dorado — not as pastiche, but as material memory.

Golden brass, mirror stainless steel, carved wood, Sierra Nevada stone, borosilicate glass, and water. Each element chosen for what it holds: weight, reflection, memory, resonance, transparency, life.

Beneath the surface, a sealed cymatics chamber, three physical controls, a real-time water system, and a holographic display transform the body of the sculpture into a living instrument.

From the Sierra to the Playa

Origin,
responsibility,
and return.

The Sierra Nevada de Santa Marta is the world's highest coastal mountain range — and one of the most sacred. The Tayrona people who have called it home for millennia developed a cosmovision in which Aluna — the invisible plane of intention and memory — precedes all material existence.

ALUNA The Frequency of Being carries that origin 4,500 miles, from the mountain to the desert. Between those two landscapes: a dialogue about what grounds us, what calls us, and what we carry between worlds.

Part of what ALUNA generates returns to its source. Through partnerships with Fundación ProSierra Nevada de Santa Marta and Siembra Consentido, resources from the project support the communities whose knowledge inspired it — through environmental recovery, circular economy initiatives, and educational infrastructure in the high Sierra.

The Tayrona aesthetic informs ALUNA's visual language. The commitment to those communities is how we honor that, not simply borrow from it.
